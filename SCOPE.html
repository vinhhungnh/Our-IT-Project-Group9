OUR SCOPE

To develop an AI that could break any tasks into smaller tasks is seemingly impossible even for AI specialists. There are tons of different work that people could do, and for each of them, there are many ways to divide big tasks into sub-steps. Creating a list of sub-steps that is efficient and easy for users to follow is also one aspect that we have to consider when developing AI algorithms. Therefore, we would start to develop the application to perform division firstly on basic tasks such as homework, assignments and things that are related to education. Our first milestone will be helping students to learn efficiently by using the application to create study plans. There would also be errors or ineffective steps in the plans, we will try to minimize that number and provide an option for the user to adjust those parts themselves. These adjustments will be monitored by the app and sent back to our servers. We will then analyze that data in order to generate patterns based on user’s behavior and improve the AI algorithms. This would lead us to the next step in performing the application in other fields of work as we will continuously collect and process data for our technology to work more and more perfectly.

RISKS

This is the first time we are in charge of a project to build a mobile app, so dealing with risks is unavoidable. In fact, identifying them is an important part of our team's awareness and improvement to avoid slowing down the project. These are some of the potential major hurdles to the project's completion.

Limited experience and knowlegde

The lack of knowledge, as well as experience, is always a big obstacle in fixing software problems. Programming in particular is a fairly new field for us as the team members are all freshmen. We all have basic knowledge as well as some experience working in Java and Python. However, applying AI technology to the application will be more complicated. As a result, we often encounter syntax errors and runtime errors that lead to application features that may not fully meet our goals. In addition, when working with many code files, it made us a bit confused and took a long time to debug. In addition, sometimes we encounter conflicts with different operating systems that lead to the application not being able to work. Furthermore, using suboptimal algorithms in code can lead to applications taking longer to start and consuming more RAM. Regarding the design session, the team members all have general knowledge of interface design but do not have much experience in practice. Specifically, we often have difficulty in designing responsive interfaces as well as elements like buttons and slide bars overlapping each other.

Cyberthreats

In addition to inside risks from software and hardware that we use to develop this application, there are some inevitable outside risks. They can specifically come from malware, third-party that users have already installed on their devices or even malicious attackers. As a result, these factors can somehow hinder our product’s working or even cause user data leaking, which is not only harmful to users but also to developers.

Team collaboration

Occasionally, disagreements among team members cause us to sit together to discuss again, and it can take longer than we expected to complete tasks. Moreover, it is not excluded that a member suddenly leaves the group, which adversely affects the project progress as well as the working efficiency of the rest.
